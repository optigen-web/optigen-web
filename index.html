OptiGen Concept

OptiGen presents a novel design that intends to revolutionise the industy by lowering the weight and cost of offshore wind turbine generators (WTGs). Our solution allows for further scalability of designs to power ratings beyond 25 MW in its current design. PENDIENTE REDACCIÓN

Lower CAPEX

OptiGen's design achieves a lighter and more stable drivetrain. This allows for a more lightweight overall supporting structure, with further savings in the nacelle, the tower and the substructure. With a more stable, tighter airgap, OptiGen also achieves a significant saving in active materials, which typically carry a relevant share of overall generator construction costs.

By means of these material reductions, OptiGen achieves a drastig reduction of overall construction costs.

By bringing the support of rotation away from the rotor's center and onto its periphery, OptiGen allows for a much increased airgap stability, even with relaxed manufacturing tolerance requirements. In turn, this allows for a reduction in the airgap length, leading to a substantial reduction of active materials when compared with other equivalent technological solutions.

As a rule of thumb, the airgap is typically set at about 1/1000th of the generator diameter. For example, for the NREL 15 MW, which has a generator with 10 m of diameter, the airgap is roughly 10 mm.
